# Investment Psychology Checklist ✅

> A pre-investment psychological checklist based on Charlie Munger's cognitive biases, Daniel Kahneman's behavioral economics, and decades of investment research.

**Before you buy or sell, run through this checklist.** It takes 2 minutes and can save you from the behavioral errors that destroy more investment returns than bad analysis ever will.

## Why This Exists

Charlie Munger identified 25 psychological tendencies that lead to poor decisions. Daniel Kahneman won a Nobel Prize for proving that humans are systematically irrational about risk and loss. The research is clear: **your psychology is your biggest investment risk.**

This checklist distills the most critical behavioral checks into a practical tool you can use before every investment decision.

## The Checklist

### Before Any Buy Decision

- [ ] **Am I within my circle of competence?** Can I explain this business's competitive advantage in simple terms? (Buffett)
- [ ] **Am I anchoring to an irrelevant number?** Am I fixated on the stock's 52-week high/low, my previous purchase price, or someone else's price target? (Kahneman)
- [ ] **Is this FOMO or analysis?** Am I buying because the price has gone up and I'm afraid of missing out? (Munger: "The world is driven by envy, not greed")
- [ ] **Have I done second-level thinking?** What does the consensus expect, and why might I be right when the market is wrong? (Howard Marks)
- [ ] **What's my margin of safety?** How much could I lose if I'm wrong? Can I tolerate that loss? (Graham/Klarman)
- [ ] **Am I being influenced by a narrative?** Great stories don't always make great investments. Am I buying the story or the numbers? (Peter Lynch)
- [ ] **Have I considered the base rate?** What percentage of companies in this situation actually succeed? (Munger: base rate neglect)
- [ ] **Am I suffering from recency bias?** Am I overweighting what happened recently and ignoring long-term patterns? (Kahneman)
- [ ] **Is this confirmation bias?** Have I actively sought out reasons NOT to buy? (Munger)
- [ ] **Would I buy the entire business at this price?** If not, why am I buying the stock? (Buffett)

### Before Any Sell Decision

- [ ] **Am I selling because of price movement or fundamental change?** Stock price drops are not the same as business deterioration. (Buffett)
- [ ] **Am I experiencing loss aversion?** Am I holding a loser because selling would make the loss "real"? (Kahneman: losses feel 2x as painful as gains)
- [ ] **Is this the disposition effect?** Am I selling winners too early to "lock in gains" while holding losers? (Shefrin & Statman)
- [ ] **Have I reviewed my original thesis?** Pull up what I wrote when I bought. Has anything actually changed? (Investment journal)
- [ ] **Am I panic selling?** If the market dropped 20% and I'm selling, I'm probably acting on fear, not analysis. (Buffett: "The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient")
- [ ] **What would I do if I didn't already own this?** Would I buy it at today's price? If yes, why sell? (Mental accounting bias)

### General Decision-Making Checks

- [ ] **Am I making this decision in a calm state?** Never make investment decisions when angry, euphoric, or fearful. (All masters)
- [ ] **Have I slept on it?** For any position > 5% of portfolio, wait 48 hours between decision and execution. (Cooling period)
- [ ] **Can I write down my reasoning in 3 sentences?** If not, I don't understand the investment well enough. (Clarity test)
- [ ] **What's the opportunity cost?** What else could I do with this capital? Is this truly the best use? (Munger)
- [ ] **Am I herding?** If everyone I know is doing this, that's a warning sign, not a confirmation. (Contrarian indicator)
- [ ] **Have I considered what I don't know?** What information would change my mind? Do I have access to it? (Marks: "Risk means more things can happen than will happen")

## The 10 Most Dangerous Cognitive Biases for Investors

| # | Bias | What It Does | How to Counter It |
|---|------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Loss Aversion** | Losses feel 2x as painful as equivalent gains | Set stop-losses in advance; review decisions rationally |
| 2 | **Anchoring** | Fixating on irrelevant reference points (purchase price, 52-week high) | Focus on intrinsic value, not price history |
| 3 | **Confirmation Bias** | Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs | Actively seek disconfirming evidence |
| 4 | **Overconfidence** | Overestimating your knowledge and predictive ability | Track your predictions; review accuracy honestly |
| 5 | **Herding** | Following the crowd into popular investments | Ask: "Would I buy this if nobody else was?" |
| 6 | **Recency Bias** | Overweighting recent events in forecasts | Study base rates and long-term historical data |
| 7 | **Disposition Effect** | Selling winners too early, holding losers too long | Judge each position on current merits, not cost basis |
| 8 | **Sunk Cost Fallacy** | Holding a bad investment because you've "already invested so much" | Only consider future prospects, not past costs |
| 9 | **Availability Bias** | Overweighting vivid, memorable events (crashes, booms) | Use data and base rates, not memorable anecdotes |
| 10 | **Status Quo Bias** | Keeping positions because change feels risky | Regularly ask: "Would I buy this today at this price?" |
